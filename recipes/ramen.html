Delicious Tantanmen Ramen

Creamy, spicy, umami soup noodles to warm the soul

This is one of my favourite dishes and always a crowd pleaser. Hearty and satisfying, with mince and slices of pork belly along with al dente noodles and a delectable spicy and creamy broth

Ingredients: (Serves 4)

  • For the sesame tare:
  • 200g roasted white sesame seeds
  • 150g regular soy sauce
  • 100g sugar
  • 100g chilli oil
  • 35g ginger, peeled and roughly cut
  • 20g Spring onion, cut into thin slivers
  • 250g sesame paste

  • For the ground pork:
  • 200g minced pork
  • 20g tobanjan (Chinese hot bean sauce)
  • 160g regular soy sauce
  • 5g chilli oil
  • 5g vegetable oil
  • 5g peeled and finely chopped garlic
  • 5g peeled and finely chopped ginger
  • 10g spring onion, finely chopped

  • To finish:
  • 1.25 litres strained hot stock
  • 110g fresh ramen noodles (or use another noodle)
  • 4 large leaves bok choy, blanched and chilled
  • Chilli oil â€“ make your own or buy a decent one
  • A thin slice or two of slow-roasted pork belly (optional)

Steps:

  1. First make the sesame tare. Place all the ingredients in a food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. Stir-fry the minced pork in the hot oil until brown, add the other ingredients and carry on cooking until good and dry. Set aside
  3. Have everything ready and laid out, so you can work quickly. Have one pot ready with hot stock hot and another with boiling water, for cook the noodles.
  4. Divide the tare between the four bowls, divide the stock equally too, then whisk until the tare has made the stock creamy.
  5. Cook the noodles, drain and divide between the four bowls. Top with the ground pork and bok choy. Finish with as much chilli as you dare, and serve.
